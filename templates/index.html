Exploring Cerebral Organoid Research in Neuroscience

Over the past ten years, cerebral organoids have transformed how we study the brain. Derived from stem cells, these 3D “mini-brains in a dish” mimic early brain development and offer an ethical, species-relevant alternative to traditional animal models. Organoids have opened new frontiers across neurodevelopment, neurodegeneration, and neuro-oncology — enabling researchers to model complex brain processes, human diseases, and even patient-specific traits in a controlled lab setting

About OrganoidMap

With OrganoidMap, we aim to provide a straightforward, broadly accessible tool suitable for everyone interested in cerebral organoids — whether you’re a beginner or an expert, this tool helps you navigate the growing and complex landscape of cerebral organoid research.

Vision

Our interactive platform brings together approximately 800 publications from the past decade, systematically categorized by research domain, cell source, application, as well as model advantages and limitations.

How to Use the Platform

You have two main ways to explore the dataset:

  1. Search Bar
    Use the search bar at the top of the page to directly look for specific terms, keywords, or topics.

  2. Filter Options
    Use the filter panel to explore and narrow down studies based on key categories:
    • Group – Neurodevelopment, Neurodegeneration, or Neuro-oncology
    • Subgroup – e.g., functional studies in neurodevelopment, tauopathies in neurodegeneration, or glioma in neuro-oncology
    • Model Type – e.g., Cerebral organoid, assembloid, or co-culture systems
    • Cell Origin – e.g., embryonic stem cells (ESCs) or induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs)

By visualizing trends and synthesizing relevant models in one table — allowing you to directly compare their advantages and limitations — we aim to support more informed, precise, and translationally relevant neuroscience.
